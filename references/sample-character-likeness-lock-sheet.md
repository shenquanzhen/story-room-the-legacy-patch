# Character Likeness Lock Sheet

## Label
- Season id: season-demo-001
- Working title: Return to the Gu Throne
- Package type: character likeness lock sheet
- Status: working visual-control package

## Purpose
This sheet exists to reduce face drift, styling drift, and role-energy drift during image generation or look-book development.

Use it before:
- key-frame generation
- visual look-book assembly
- anchor-episode concept art
- moodboard or pitch-image passes

## Global Likeness Rules
- Keep all four core characters in a premium modern Chinese urban-drama realism lane.
- Avoid fantasy beauty standards, overly westernized face drift, idol-poster overglam, and soap-opera over-softening.
- Skin texture should remain realistic.
- Wardrobe can change by scene, but facial structure, age read, eye energy, and posture identity should remain stable.
- Expressions should stay role-consistent: power comes from control, not caricature.

---

## 1. Lin Yue
### Role Function
- Fallen heiress -> returning power center
- Must read as intelligent before she reads as emotional
- The audience should believe she remembers more than she says

### Age / Presence Band
- Late 20s to early 30s
- Elegant, disciplined, not girlish
- Reads as someone born into elite space even when dressed down

### Facial Identity Anchors
- Oval-to-slightly narrow face shape
- Clean jawline, refined cheek structure, not overly sharp
- Eyes steady, observant, slightly cool, high restraint
- Brows defined but not aggressive
- Nose straight and refined
- Mouth controlled; more often held than smiling

### Hair Identity Anchors
- Dark natural black or near-black hair
- Usually clean, straight, or softly controlled styling
- No overly ornate curls, fantasy hairpieces, or trendy idol styling

### Expression Rules
- Default = composed, withholding, intelligent
- Pain should read as internalized severance, not collapse
- Victory should read as stillness, not gloating
- Anger should sharpen the gaze, not explode into theatrical distortion

### Wardrobe Rules
- Even in service uniform, she should still feel innately high-status
- Later formalwear should be clean, minimal, structurally elegant
- Avoid excessive jewelry, influencer styling, or soft-romance heroine looks

### Negative Drift To Avoid
- overly cute / sweet ingénue face
- glamorous influencer makeup look
- fantasy empress styling
- exaggerated sobbing melodrama
- generic action-heroine hardness

### Short Prompt Anchor
Poised young Chinese woman, late 20s to early 30s, refined oval face, straight dark hair, calm observant eyes, emotionally restrained, premium urban drama realism, elegance through control not ornament

---

## 2. Gu Nancheng
### Role Function
- Visible heir and front-facing antagonist
- Must read as polished entitlement rather than brute force
- His danger comes from confidence, grooming, and borrowed legitimacy

### Age / Presence Band
- Early 30s
- Attractive in a socially dominant, curated way
- Must feel expensive, not rugged

### Facial Identity Anchors
- Clean structured face, slightly sharper than Lin Yue's
- Controlled eyes with confidence bordering on contempt
- Brows neat, self-managed, not wild
- Nose and jaw should feel polished rather than rough
- Smile should often read as practiced, not warm

### Hair Identity Anchors
- Dark hair, well styled, elite-business grooming
- No messy rebel hair, no overly soft idol fringe

### Expression Rules
- Default = composed superiority
- Cruelty should feel socially polished
- Panic moments should read as control slipping, not full breakdown
- Romance-mode tenderness should still feel performative and managerial

### Wardrobe Rules
- Tailored suits, elite eventwear, luxury understatement
- He should look like someone who grew into visible power without earning moral authority
- Avoid gangster styling, streetwear aggression, or flamboyant rich-boy excess

### Negative Drift To Avoid
- rugged bad-boy aesthetic
- gangster boss energy
- soft innocent male lead vibe
- cartoon villain sneer
- overbuilt alpha-male physique emphasis

### Short Prompt Anchor
Polished young Chinese elite man, early 30s, tailored grooming, controlled contemptuous eyes, expensive suit energy, socially dominant, handsome but emotionally cold, premium urban family-power drama realism

---

## 3. Patriarch
### Role Function
- Highest authority lane
- Must read as consequential, not merely old
- His ambiguity is a structural asset; he cannot look purely kind or purely monstrous

### Age / Presence Band
- Late 50s to late 60s
- Strong elder presence, controlled authority
- Should feel like someone whose silence can move institutions

### Facial Identity Anchors
- Mature face with clear age lines, not frail
- Eyes heavy with unreadable history
- Brows and mouth controlled, minimal expressive waste
- Strong nose / authoritative profile
- Face should carry burden and calculation together

### Hair Identity Anchors
- Dark-to-gray formal hair, disciplined styling
- No theatrical white-hair patriarch fantasy look

### Expression Rules
- Default = unreadable, evaluative, consequential
- Concern should never look sentimental too early
- Silence should feel active
- Even when kneeling, he should look like a man forced by power logic, not reduced to weakness alone

### Wardrobe Rules
- Formal, elite, conservative, institution-heavy
- Should visually anchor the upper authority world
- Avoid old-money caricature costume excess

### Negative Drift To Avoid
- gentle grandfather softness
- villain-monster grotesque energy
- fantasy clan elder styling
- frail hospital-patient elder look
- melodramatic crying patriarch

### Short Prompt Anchor
Powerful older Chinese patriarch, late 50s to late 60s, strong lined face, unreadable eyes, disciplined gray-black hair, elite formal authority, silence with institutional weight, premium urban drama realism

---

## 4. Ally
### Role Function
- Useful but dangerous support vector
- Must read as elegant, intelligent, and withholding
- Never fully safe, never generic helper

### Age / Presence Band
- Early 30s to late 30s
- Refined, self-possessed, socially fluent
- Could be read as ally or hidden risk in the same glance

### Facial Identity Anchors
- Refined face with controlled softness hiding calculation
- Eyes perceptive, guarded, slightly tired from history
- Features elegant rather than flashy
- Expression should suggest selective truth and private damage

### Hair Identity Anchors
- Dark hair, clean, understated styling
- No flamboyant fashion-editorial look

### Expression Rules
- Default = calm, measured, unreadable warmth
- Helpfulness should never fully erase danger
- Pain should read as old and managed, not immediate and exposed
- Even honest moments should feel partial

### Wardrobe Rules
- Elegant urban tailoring, understated wealth, neutral palette
- Should sit visually between Lin Yue's discipline and Gu Nancheng's polished entitlement
- Avoid obvious romantic-lead softening or mysterious-villain costume coding

### Negative Drift To Avoid
- pure romantic male-lead prettiness
- obviously villainous shadow styling
- flashy fashion influencer styling
- generic bodyguard / fixer look
- warm trustworthy helper default

### Short Prompt Anchor
Elegant Chinese man, early 30s to late 30s, refined and guarded, perceptive tired eyes, understated tailoring, useful but dangerous, selective warmth, premium urban intrigue realism

## Cross-Character Separation Rules
### Lin Yue vs Ally
- Lin Yue = cleaner steel, more central moral force
- Ally = softer surface, more ambiguity, more hidden compromise

### Gu Nancheng vs Ally
- Gu Nancheng = entitlement and public polish
- Ally = restraint and private knowledge

### Patriarch vs Gu Nancheng
- Patriarch = institutional gravity
- Gu Nancheng = borrowed authority and performative control

## Consistency Check Before Image Generation
For every generated image, ask:
1. Does Lin Yue still look like the same person across humiliation, midpoint, and finale?
2. Does Gu Nancheng still look socially polished rather than physically brutish?
3. Does the patriarch still read as active ambiguity rather than soft elder or obvious villain?
4. Does the ally still read as useful but unsafe?
5. Did any character drift into fantasy, idol-poster, or soap-opera exaggeration?
